Sa11ytaire

What is it?

Sa11ytaire is a solitaire app that has been developed to work with accessibility tools such as Narrator, Windows Speech Recognition, keyboard shortcuts, and other interaction models.  It plays like a regular solitaire game but is extremely focused on giving customers with disabilities a wonderful experience as well.

 

 

How do I play?

Your goal is to get all the cards in order from Ace to King into the four suit piles (Clubs, Diamonds, Hearts, and Spades respectively).  Select the Next card button to bring up a list of cards in the deck.  You can then take cards that are face up in the deck and put them on the gameboard below.  Here is an example of a few moves you can make:

·       Put the 9 of Spades or 9 of Clubs below the 10 of Diamonds

·       Put the 6 of Spades or 6 of Clubs below the 7 of Diamonds

·       Put the 6 of Hearts or 6 of Diamonds below the 7 of Clubs

 

Red over Black; and Black over Red

Hearts and Diamonds are red-suited cards, while Clubs and Spades are black-suited cards.  You can only put red-suited cards below black-suited cards, and black-suited cards below red-suited cards.  They also must be one number below as shown in the example above.

Additionally, as you move cards around the board, you will expose cards that are facedown.  For example, if the 2 of Hearts were moved in front of the 3 of Spades that card which was face-down behind it would then become face-up.

 

Kings are Key

Once a card pile becomes empty, you can only move a King over to it.  It can be any suit, but it must be a King.  Additionally, if you have series of cards with the King (say from King all the way down to 3) those cards will also move with it to the empty pile.  This is an excellent way to free up space on the gameboard. 

 

Sa11yTaire Keyboard Usage

Here is a list of some keyboard shortcuts you can use with Sa11ytaire:

·       Press Tab or Right Arrow to move between elements.

·       Press Shift + Tab or Left Arrow to move backwards between elements.

·       Press Up Arrow or Down Arrow to move between face-up cards in a dealt card pile. Note that Up Arrow and Down Arrow will not move to a face-down card.

·       Press Esc to unselect all cards.

·       Press Alt + N to turn over the next remaining cards.

·       Press Alt + U to toggle an upturned card to checked.

·       Press Alt + C/D/H/S to toggle the checked Target Cards pile for either Clubs, Diamonds, Hearts, or Spades. 

·       Press Alt + 1/2/3/4/5/6/7 to select the top card in one of the 7 card piles on the screen. 

·       Press F1 to show this help content.

·       Press F5 to restart the game.

·       Press F6 to move between the main areas of the remaining cards, the target cards, and the dealt cards.

·       Press F8 to turn on a mode where a card moves automatically if a move is available, as soon as the card is selected.

·       Press F9 to turn on a mode where cards can be selected via their access keys, even when the Alt key is not pressed.

·       Press F10 to turn on or off switch mode.  This is where a game can be played with a switch device that emulates Spacebar presses. 

 

Narrator Keyboard Shortcuts

The following are some examples of keyboard shortcuts you can use while Narrator is running:

·       Press F2 to announce the details of the remaining card piles.

·       Press F3 to announce the details of the target card piles.

·       Press F4 to announce the details of the dealt card piles.

·       Press F7 to announce hints on moves currently available between dealt card piles.

 

Play with a Xbox controller

·       D-Pad: Moves left/right/up/down through the app.

·       A Button: Invoke the Next Card button, or check the Upturned Card button or Target Card Pile buttons, or select an item in the Dealt Card Pile lists.

·       LeftThumbstick Button: Replicates A button action, in order for the game to be playable with controls only on the left side of the controller.

·       RightThumbstick Button: Restart game.

·       X Button: Deselect and uncheck everything.

·       Y Button: Toggle the state of switch device control.

·       Right Bumper: If switch device control is not enabled, moves to the first card in the three main areas in the app. If switch device control is enabled, then triggers the switch device action in the app.

·       Left Bumper: Turn on a mode where cards move automatically if a move is available, as soon as the card is selected.

 

Note: If a bumper has been pressed on an Xbox controller, then the game's keyboard access keys can be triggered with a chatpad. By default, these keys are N, U, C, D, H, S, 1/2/3/4/5/6/7.

 

For having Narrator announce details of the game on a Xbox

·       RightThumbstick Left/Up/Right: Have Narrator announce the state of the remaining cards, target card piles, or dealt card piles respectively.

·       RightThumbstick Down: Have Narrator announce the hint on whether a card can be moved between the dealt card pile lists.
